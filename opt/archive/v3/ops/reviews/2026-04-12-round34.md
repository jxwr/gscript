#### Gap #1 — Target concentration: `tier2_float_loop` is burning rounds (user 12:51)

10 of the last 15 rounds targeted `tier2_float_loop`; 6 were `no_change`. The user noticed (12:51) and it's a local-optimum signal that the harness isn't structurally breaking out of. The Ceiling Rule DOES block after 2 consecutive failures, but `ceiling_decay` undoes it after 3 rounds absent — which is exactly what happened to `tier1_dispatch` (R33 decay, R34 would have been back on it). Pattern: decay schedules the same problem to come back, not to be escaped.

**Proposed fix — "forced diversification" rule**: ANALYZE Step 1 must pick a category NOT in the last N rounds' top category, if that category has `category_failures >= 1`. Concretely: if `tier2_float_loop` has `category_failures >= 1` AND 3 of the last 5 rounds targeted it, ANALYZE must pick a different category. One of the excluded-from-reference-baseline targets (object_creation, sort) is a natural alternative and maps to the R7-flagged drift above.

**Source / confidence**: internal observation (R20-R34 target history), `confidence: MEDIUM` — diversification is a common local-search escape (simulated annealing, tabu search) but the specific parametrization is a guess.
